Case study: Medical expenditures with one endogenous regressor

Dependent variable:

ldrugexp, the log of total out-of-pocket expenditures on prescribed medications.

The regressors:

whether the individual holds either employer or union-sponsored health insurance (hi_empunion)

number of chronic conditions (totchr)

age in years (age), whether female (female) whether black or Hispanic (blhisp ) and the natural logarithm of annual household income in thousands of dollars (linc)

Endogenous variable: hi_empunion

Available instruments:

ssiratio: the ratio of an individual's social security income to the individual's income from all sources, with high values indicating a significant income constraint.

firmsz: the size of t he firm's employed labor force

Relevance: (1)ssiratio is expected to be negatively correlated with having supplementary insurance (hi_empunion).

(2)firmsz intended to capture whether the individual has access to supplementary insurance through the employer.

Exogeneity: (1)ssiratio is orthogonal to error term (u_i). Actually, we can assume that the direct role of income is adequately captured by the regressor linc. Therefore nothing in u_i is related to ssiratio.

(2)However, firmsz may be related to the intention of individuals to obtain supplementary insurance, which is the omitted variable hiding in u_i. The OIR test will help to show this.

Within of FE estimator with cluster-robust standard errors

\[(y_{it}-\bar{y}_i+\bar{\bar{y}})=\alpha+(x_{it}-\bar{x}_i+\bar{\bar{x}})'{\beta}+({\epsilon}_{it}-\bar{{\epsilon}}_i+\bar{\bar{{\epsilon}}})\]

\[(y_{it}-\bar{y}_i+\bar{\bar{y}})=\alpha+(x_{it}-\bar{x}_i+\bar{\bar{x}})'{\beta}+({\epsilon}_{it}-\bar{{\epsilon}}_i+\bar{\bar{{\epsilon}}})\] The \(R^2\) from the mean-deviated regression: \[Within R^2: {\rho}^{2}\{({y}_{it}-{\bar{y}}_{i}),({x'}_{it}{\hat{\beta}}-{\bar{x}}'_{it}{\hat{\beta}})\}\] The combined error in the individual-effects model that we label \(\alpha_i +\epsilon_i\) is referred to as \(u_i + e_i\) in the Stata documentation and output.Thus Stata output sigma_u gives the standard deviation of the individual effect \(\alpha\), and sigma_e gives the standard deviation of \(\epsilon\)·

Example 1

beer(1984(1)1988):The beer variable has the time period (1984(1)1988) specified, meaning that it is averaged for the periods 1984,1985,...,1988.

cigsale(1988) cigsale(1980) cigsale(1975): The variable cigsale will be used three times as a predictor using the values from periods 1988, 1980, and 1975 respectively.

trunit(3):the unit affected by the intervention is unit no 3 (California) in the year 1989.

counit():The donor pool (since no counit() is specified) defaults to the control units 1,2,4,5,...,39 ( ie. the other 38 states in the dataset).

xperiod():Since no xperiod() is provided, the predictor variables for which no variable specific time period is specified (retprice, lnincome, and age15to24) are averaged over the entire pre-intervention period up to the year of the intervention (1970,1981,...,1988).

mspeperiod(): The MSPE is minimized over the entire pretreatment period, because mspeperiod() is not provided.

By default, results are displayed for the period from 1970,1971,...,2000 period (the earliest and latest year in the dataset).

A results figure is also requested using the fig option.
